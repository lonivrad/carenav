## medicare-home-health#00-overview-eligibility

*Program: medicare-home-health · Heading: Overview / Eligibility*

# Medicare home health benefit

Medicare Part A and/or Part B cover part-time skilled care delivered at home by
a Medicare-certified home health agency, at no cost to the beneficiary for the
covered services themselves.

## Eligibility

All of the following are required:

- Need for part-time or intermittent skilled services. A person who needs more
  than part-time or intermittent skilled care does not qualify.
- Homebound status — both conditions: (1) leaving home is not recommended
  because of the condition, or the person has trouble leaving without help
  (cane, wheelchair, walker, crutches, special transportation, or another
  person) because of illness or injury; and (2) the person is normally unable
  to leave home and leaving takes considerable effort. Permitted absences:
  medical treatment and short or infrequent non-medical trips (for example,
  religious services); attending adult day care does not disqualify.
- A face-to-face assessment by a health care provider (physician or, for
  example, a nurse practitioner) before certification of need.
- The care must be ordered by a health care provider and delivered by a
  Medicare-certified home health agency.

## medicare-home-health#01-what-it-covers-how-to-apply

*Program: medicare-home-health · Heading: What it covers / How to apply*

## What it covers

- Medically necessary part-time or intermittent skilled nursing care: wound
  care for pressure sores or surgical wounds, patient and caregiver education,
  IV or nutrition therapy, injections, and monitoring of serious illness or
  unstable health status.
- Physical therapy, occupational therapy, and speech-language pathology
  services (subject to conditions).
- Medical social services.
- Part-time or intermittent home health aide care (help walking, bathing and
  grooming, changing bed linens, feeding) — only while also receiving skilled
  nursing, PT, SLP, or OT.
- Injectable osteoporosis drugs for women meeting certain criteria.
- Durable medical equipment (DME) and medical supplies for home use.
- Frequency: no cap on visits while qualified; "part-time or intermittent"
  generally means skilled nursing plus aide services combined up to 8 hours a
  day and 28 hours a week (up to 35 hours a week for a short time if the
  provider deems it necessary).
- Not covered: 24-hour-a-day care at home, home meal delivery, homemaker
  services unrelated to the care plan, and custodial or personal care when it
  is the only care needed.
- Costs: $0 for covered home health services. For Medicare-covered DME, 20%
  of the Medicare-approved amount after the Part B deductible ($283/year in
  2026).

## How to apply

1. A provider performs the face-to-face assessment and orders the care.
2. The provider supplies a list of Medicare-certified agencies serving the
   area (and must disclose any financial interest in agencies listed).
3. The agency schedules an appointment, discusses needs, and coordinates the
   care plan with the doctor.
4. Before care starts, the agency must state — verbally and in writing — what
   Medicare will and will not pay, and must issue an Advance Beneficiary
   Notice (ABN) before providing non-covered items or services.

## medicare-home-health#02-documentation-needed

*Program: medicare-home-health · Heading: Documentation needed*

## Documentation needed

- Face-to-face assessment and provider certification of homebound status and
  need for skilled care.
- Provider order for home health services and the agency-maintained care plan.
- Medicare.gov does not publish a beneficiary paperwork checklist — the
  agency and provider assemble the certification records. Items agencies
  commonly involve the beneficiary in (manually compiled 2026-07-05; varies
  by agency, so confirm at intake):
  - The face-to-face encounter note (a visit with the certifying provider
    within 90 days before or 30 days after care starts) and the written
    homebound-status justification.
  - The plan of care (CMS-485), which the beneficiary's doctor must sign,
    listing ordered services, frequency, and duration.
  - In Washington: a copy of the POLST for the agency binder, with the
    original bright-green form posted visibly in the home for EMS; health
    care directive and power-of-attorney documents if someone else signs
    consents (surrogate hierarchy under RCW 7.70.065 applies without a
    DPOA).
  - Supplemental coverage documents (Medigap, Medicaid, or LTC insurance),
    and the Home Health Change of Care Notice (HHCCN) or ABN if the agency
    expects Medicare to deny particular services.

## medicare-snf#00-overview-eligibility

*Program: medicare-snf · Heading: Overview / Eligibility*

# Medicare skilled nursing facility (SNF) coverage

Medicare Part A covers short-term skilled nursing and rehabilitation care in a
Medicare-certified skilled nursing facility after a qualifying hospital stay —
up to 100 days per benefit period. It is not a long-term custodial care
benefit.

## Eligibility

All of the following are required:

- Medicare Part A with days remaining in the benefit period.
- A qualifying inpatient hospital stay: a medically necessary inpatient stay
  of at least 3 days in a row, counting the admission day but not the
  discharge day. Time under observation status or in the emergency room before
  formal admission does not count, even overnight. Exceptions: a doctor in an
  ACO with an approved SNF 3-Day Rule Waiver; some Medicare Advantage plans
  waive the 3-day stay. Status changes from inpatient to observation can be
  appealed.
- Admission to the SNF within a short time — generally 30 days — of leaving
  the hospital.
- A doctor's determination that daily skilled care is needed (for example, IV
  fluids or medications, or physical therapy) given by or under the
  supervision of skilled nursing or therapy staff.
- Care in a Medicare-certified SNF.
- The skilled services must be for an ongoing condition also treated during
  the qualifying stay (even if it was not the admission reason) or a new
  condition that started during SNF care; skilled care may aim to improve or
  maintain the condition, or prevent or delay worsening.

## medicare-snf#01-what-it-covers-how-to-apply

*Program: medicare-snf · Heading: What it covers / How to apply*

## What it covers

- Semi-private room and meals.
- Skilled nursing care.
- Physical therapy, occupational therapy, and speech-language pathology when
  needed for the health goal.
- Medical social services, medications, and medical supplies and equipment
  used in the facility.
- Ambulance transportation (when other transport would endanger health) to
  the nearest supplier of needed services not available at the SNF.
- Dietary counseling.

Costs per benefit period (2026, per CMS MM14279 and Medicare.gov):

- Part A inpatient hospital deductible: $1,736 (not paid again for SNF if
  already paid for hospital care in the same benefit period).
- Days 1–20: $0 per day (Medicare Advantage plans may charge copays).
- Days 21–100: $217 per day coinsurance.
- Days 101 and beyond: the patient pays all costs. Coverage is limited to 100
  days per benefit period.
- Benefit period definition (42 CFR 409.60): "The initial benefit period
  begins on the day the beneficiary receives inpatient hospital, inpatient
  CAH, or SNF services for the first time after becoming entitled to
  hospital insurance," and "a benefit period ends when a beneficiary has,
  for at least 60 consecutive days," not been an inpatient in a hospital,
  critical access hospital, or SNF. A new benefit period begins with the
  next inpatient admission after that 60-day gap.

## How to apply

- SNF care must follow the qualifying hospital stay. If the hospital has its
  own SNF with a bed available, the patient may stay there; otherwise find a
  bed at a separate Medicare-certified facility (many nursing homes offer SNF
  care).
- Ask the doctor or hospital discharge staff whether Medicare will cover the
  SNF stay before admission.
- Without a qualifying stay, alternatives to ask about include Medicare home
  health, Medicaid, and Veterans' benefits.

## medicare-snf#02-documentation-needed

*Program: medicare-snf · Heading: Documentation needed*

## Documentation needed

- Proof of the 3-day qualifying inpatient hospital stay (inpatient status,
  not observation).
- The doctor's order or determination of the need for daily skilled care.
- Medicare.gov does not publish a beneficiary document checklist — the
  hospital and SNF handle the certification paperwork. Items facilities
  commonly require (manually compiled 2026-07-05; varies by facility, so
  confirm with the admissions office):
  - Hospital discharge summary proving the 3-day consecutive inpatient stay
    (observation status does not count), the physician's order for daily
    skilled care, recent history & physical, a certified current medication
    list, and often proof of negative TB status.
  - Washington-specific: a completed PASRR Level I screening (DSHS 14-300)
    must accompany admission; DSHS intake/referral form 10-570 if a
    transition to Apple Health LTSS is possible; and a prior-authorization
    letter from the plan if the person is on Medicare Advantage.
  - Care directives and authority: POLST (Washington's bright-green WSMA/
    DOH form, signed by the patient/proxy and a WA-licensed clinician) or
    health care directive; durable power of attorney for health care, or
    documentation of the surrogate hierarchy under RCW 7.70.065 if there is
    no DPOA.
  - Secondary coverage for the day-21+ coinsurance: Medigap policy,
    Medicaid award letter (dual-eligibles), or LTC insurance policy; expect
    to sign the SNF Advance Beneficiary Notice (SNFABN) if the facility
    believes Medicare may deny specific services.

## va-aid-attendance#00-overview-eligibility

*Program: va-aid-attendance · Heading: Overview / Eligibility*

# VA Aid & Attendance (increased monthly pension)

Aid & Attendance (A&A) is an increased monthly pension amount added to a VA
pension for veterans (and some survivors) who need help with daily activities.
It cannot be received at the same time as the VA Housebound benefit — a
claimant gets one or the other.

## Eligibility

Two layers: the person must qualify for the underlying VA pension, and meet at
least one A&A clinical criterion.

Underlying Veterans Pension:

- Discharge that was not dishonorable.
- Income and net worth within limits set by Congress; the net worth limit is
  $163,699 (effective December 1, 2025).
- Service requirement: at least 90 days of active duty with at least 1 day
  during a recognized wartime period (if service began before September 8,
  1980); or at least 24 months / the full called period with at least 1 day
  during wartime (if enlisted after September 7, 1980); or officer rules for
  active duty starting after October 16, 1981.
- And at least one of: age 65 or older; permanent and total disability;
  long-term nursing home residence due to disability; receiving SSDI or SSI.
- Recognized wartime periods include WWII (12/7/1941–12/31/1946), Korean
  Conflict (6/27/1950–1/31/1955), Vietnam era (8/5/1964–5/7/1975, earlier for
  in-country service), and Gulf War (8/2/1990–ongoing).

A&A clinical criteria — at least one must apply:

- Needs another person to help perform daily activities such as bathing,
  feeding, or dressing.
- Has to stay in bed, or spends a large portion of the day in bed, because of
  illness.
- Is a patient in a nursing home due to loss of mental or physical abilities
  related to a disability.
- Eyesight limited to 5/200 or less in both eyes, or concentric contraction of
  the visual field to 5 degrees or less.

## va-aid-attendance#01-what-it-covers

*Program: va-aid-attendance · Heading: What it covers*

## What it covers

- A monthly cash payment added to the VA pension — not a service program. The
  money can be used for care costs at the household's discretion.
- Maximum Annual Pension Rate (MAPR) with A&A, effective December 1, 2025: the
  VA pays the difference between countable income and MAPR, divided into
  monthly payments.
  - Veteran with no dependents, with A&A: $29,093/year (versus $17,441
    standard).
  - Veteran with 1 dependent, with A&A: $34,488/year (versus $22,839
    standard).
  - Add $2,984 per additional dependent.
- Per VA's rates page, "We'll base your payment amount on the difference
  between your income for VA purposes and a limit that Congress sets
  (called the Maximum Annual Pension Rate, or MAPR)." VA publishes annual
  MAPR figures; per-month dollar amounts quoted elsewhere are derived from
  the annual figures, not published by VA.
- Surviving spouses: VA's Survivors Pension "offers monthly payments to
  qualified surviving spouses and unmarried dependent children of wartime
  Veterans who meet certain income and net worth limits set by Congress."
  Survivor MAPR with Aid & Attendance, no dependents: $18,697 (effective
  December 1, 2025).

## va-aid-attendance#02-how-to-apply

*Program: va-aid-attendance · Heading: How to apply*

## How to apply

- Underlying pension: VA Form 21P-527EZ — file online at VA.gov, upload via
  AccessVA QuickSubmit, mail, in person at a VA regional office, or through an
  accredited attorney, claims agent, or Veterans Service Organization. An
  intent-to-file is recommended for paper filers to preserve the effective
  date.
- A&A add-on: VA Form 21-2680 (Examination for Housebound Status or Permanent
  Need for Regular Aid and Attendance), with the examination section completed
  by a medical examiner.
- Mail applications to: Department of Veterans Affairs, Pension Intake Center,
  PO Box 5365, Janesville, WI 53547-5365. In person: any VA regional office.

## va-aid-attendance#03-documentation-needed

*Program: va-aid-attendance · Heading: Documentation needed*

## Documentation needed

- VA Form 21-2680 completed by a medical examiner.
- VA Form 21-0779 (Request for Nursing Home Information) if the person is in a
  nursing home.
- For the underlying pension: Social Security number, VA file number, military
  service history, marital and work history, dependent information, gross
  monthly household income, asset values, and unreimbursed medical expenses;
  medical records if under age 65.
- Unreimbursed care-cost evidence (manually verified 2026-07-05): care-cost
  evidence is part of the pension's income calculation rather than a separate
  A&A requirement. If household income is already below the MAPR, it is not
  needed to qualify (though reporting expenses can increase the payment); if
  income is above the MAPR, unreimbursed medical/care expenses (home health
  aides, assisted living, nursing home fees) are deducted to reduce
  "countable income" and are reported on VA Form 21P-8416 (Medical Expense
  Report). Typical proof: a care contract or provider statement, a breakdown
  showing the care covers activities of daily living, and receipts or
  canceled checks showing the costs were unreimbursed. Neither VA Form
  21-2680 nor 21-0779 collects expense amounts (confirmed against the
  current forms, OMB 2900-0721 exp. 02/28/2026 and OMB 2900-0652 exp.
  09/30/2026) — expenses go on 21P-8416 or the 21P-527EZ application
  itself.

## va-housebound#00-overview-eligibility-what-it-covers-how-to-apply-documentation-needed-part-1

*Program: va-housebound · Heading: Overview / Eligibility / What it covers / How to apply / Documentation needed (part 1)*

# VA Housebound benefit (increased monthly pension)

The Housebound benefit is an increased monthly pension amount for veterans who
are substantially confined to their home because of a permanent disability. It
cannot be received at the same time as VA Aid & Attendance — a claimant gets
one or the other.

## Eligibility

- Must receive (or qualify for) a VA pension. The underlying pension
  eligibility — discharge status, income and net worth limits (net worth limit
  $163,699 effective December 1, 2025), wartime service requirements, and
  age/disability criteria — is the same as described in the VA Aid &
  Attendance document (see va-aid-attendance).
- Clinical criterion (VA's plain-language standard): the person spends most of
  their time in their home because of a permanent disability.
- Regulatory definition (38 CFR 3.351(d)): applies to a veteran rated
  permanently and totally disabled who either "(1) Has additional disability
  or disabilities independently ratable at 60 percent or more, separate and
  distinct from the permanent disability rated as 100 percent disabling and
  involving different anatomical segments or bodily systems, or (2) Is
  'permanently housebound' by reason of disability or disabilities...
  substantially confined to his or her dwelling and the immediate premises
  or, if institutionalized, to the ward or clinical area, and it is
  reasonably certain that the disability or disabilities and resultant
  confinement will continue throughout his or her lifetime."

## va-housebound#01-overview-eligibility-what-it-covers-how-to-apply-documentation-needed-part-2

*Program: va-housebound · Heading: Overview / Eligibility / What it covers / How to apply / Documentation needed (part 2)*

## What it covers

- A monthly cash payment added to the VA pension.
- Maximum Annual Pension Rate (MAPR) with Housebound status, effective
  December 1, 2025:
  - Veteran with no dependents: $21,313/year (versus $17,441 standard).
  - Veteran with 1 dependent: $26,710/year (versus $22,839 standard).
  - Add $2,984 per additional dependent.
- Two veterans married to each other (MAPR, effective December 1, 2025):
  one qualifies for Housebound: $26,710/year; both qualify for Housebound:
  $30,580/year; one qualifies for Housebound and one for Aid & Attendance:
  $38,350/year.

## How to apply

- Same process and forms as Aid & Attendance: underlying pension via VA Form
  21P-527EZ; Housebound status documented with VA Form 21-2680 (the same form
  covers both Housebound and A&A examinations).
- Submit online, by mail to: Department of Veterans Affairs, Pension Intake
  Center, PO Box 5365, Janesville, WI 53547-5365, or in person at a VA
  regional office.

## Documentation needed

- VA Form 21-2680 with the examination section completed by a medical
  examiner, documenting housebound status.
- VA Form 21-0779 (Request for Nursing Home Information) if in a nursing home.
- Underlying pension documentation: Social Security number, VA file number,
  military service history, marital and work history, dependent information,
  income, assets, and unreimbursed medical expenses.

## wa-apple-health-ltc#00-overview-eligibility-part-1

*Program: wa-apple-health-ltc · Heading: Overview / Eligibility (part 1)*

# Apple Health long-term care (institutional/LTSS Medicaid)

Washington's Medicaid coverage for long-term services and supports — nursing
facility care and the financial gateway to HCS waivers (COPES, New Freedom,
Residential Support), PACE, hospice in an institution, and community LTSS.
Administered by the Health Care Authority (financial) and DSHS Home and
Community Services (functional and case management).

## Eligibility

General (WAC 182-513-1316/-1317):

- Meet Apple Health general requirements (WAC 182-503-0505), including
  citizenship/immigration rules.
- Attain institutional status (WAC 182-513-1320, effective 2/17/2017): in a
  medical institution, "a person must reside in a medical institution thirty
  consecutive days or more, or based on a department assessment, be likely
  to reside in a medical institution thirty consecutive days or more";
  outside an institution, status comes from receiving HCB waiver services
  (chapter 182-515 WAC) or equivalent programs (RCL, PACE, hospice, or
  state-funded LTC services).
- Meet functional eligibility — for HCS clients, nursing facility level of
  care via the CARE assessment (chapter 388-106 WAC).
- Meet SSI-related criteria (WAC 182-512-0050) or MAGI-based criteria if
  residing in a medical institution.

Income:

- SSI-related CN: available income at or below the Special Income Level
  (SIL), equal to 300% of the SSI federal benefit rate — $2,982/month
  (1/1/2026; FBR $994 single / $1,491 couple).
- Above the SIL: Medically Needy pathway (MNIL $994 single, 1/1/2026).
  Mechanics per WAC 182-513-1395 (institutional MN, effective 2/17/2017):
  a person whose remaining income exceeds the state-contracted daily rate
  but falls below the private nursing facility rate "is eligible to receive
  institutional services at the state-contracted rate," approved for a
  three- or six-month base period, with MN eligibility when "additional
  medical expenses incurred during the base period exceeds the total
  remaining income for all months of the base period minus the total
  state-contracted rate." Current private nursing facility rate: $462/day,
  $14,059/month (10/1/2025).
- MAGI-based institutional coverage (chapter 182-514 WAC) has no asset test.

## wa-apple-health-ltc#01-overview-eligibility-part-2

*Program: wa-apple-health-ltc · Heading: Overview / Eligibility (part 2)*

Resources (SSI-related):

- $2,000 single / $3,000 couple; home equity limit $1,130,000 (1/1/2026) —
  exceeding it bars LTC coverage.
- Spousal impoverishment: community spouse resource allowance — state amount
  $72,529 (7/1/2025), federal maximum $162,660 (1/1/2026); community spouse
  monthly maintenance needs allowance maximum $4,066.50 (1/1/2026); dependent
  allowance $2,644 (7/1/2025). Resources above the $2,000 limit must be
  transferred to the community spouse within one year.
- 60-month look-back for asset transfers (WAC 182-513-1363): uncompensated
  transfers are presumed disqualifying unless rebutted or excepted (transfers
  to a spouse, to a blind or disabled child, sole-benefit trusts for a
  disabled person under 65, fair-market-value sales). Annuities must be
  disclosed (chapter 182-516 WAC).

Post-eligibility (cost of care): institutionalized clients pay income toward
cost of care minus a personal needs allowance — $41.62 (clothing/personal
incidentals) or $108.74 ("all other PNA, medical institutions," 1/1/2026);
State Veterans Home max $160. A housing maintenance allowance (up to 100% FPL,
$1,330, 4/1/2026) may be allowed to hold a residence.
Which PNA applies depends on setting and payment source (WAC 182-513-1105,
effective 12/5/2025, WSR 25-22-083 — separate amounts for medical
institutions vs. alternate living facilities and by funding source); "the
current PNA and room and board standards used in long-term services and
supports are published under the institutional standards on the Washington
apple health (medicaid) income and resource standards chart located at
www.hca.wa.gov."

## How to apply

Financial application (any one):

  Medicare).
- Online: wahealthplanfinder.org (under 65, no Medicare; flag the LTSS need

- Paper: HCA 18-005 "Washington Apple Health Application for Aged, Blind,
  Disabled / Long-Term Care" — mail or fax to Home and Community Services –
  LTSS, PO Box 45826, Olympia, WA 98504-5826; fax 1-855-635-8305.
- By phone via the DSHS customer service contact center, or in person at any
  HCS office or Community Services Office.

Functional assessment — call HCS central intake:

- Region 1 (Eastern WA, incl. Spokane, Yakima, Tri-Cities): 509-568-3767 or
  866-323-9409.
- Region 2 (Island, San Juan, Skagit, Snohomish, Whatcom, parts of North
  King): 800-780-7094; King County other ZIPs: 206-341-7750.
- Region 3 (Pierce, Kitsap, Thurston, Clark, Olympic Peninsula / SW WA):
  800-786-3799.

Process: (1) application; (2) financial review plus a required phone or

established and an approved provider is in place. Processing standard: 45
calendar days (60 if a disability decision is required, WAC 182-503-0060).
Denials carry written appeal rights. An authorized representative can be
added (DSHS 14-532). A "Fast Track" service authorization pending financial
determination exists.

## wa-cares-fund#00-overview-eligibility

*Program: wa-cares-fund · Heading: Overview / Eligibility*

# WA Cares Fund

Washington's public long-term care insurance program, funded by a 0.58% payroll
premium on Washington workers' wages. Contributions began July 1, 2023;
benefits became available statewide as of July 1, 2026 (the site banner now
reads "Benefits now available statewide").

## Eligibility

Contribution (vesting) pathways — a worker must meet one:

1. Permanent: contribute for a total of 10 years "without interruption of
   five or more consecutive years" (RCW 50B.04.050) — the wacaresfund.wa.gov
   pages summarize this as "at least 10 years." Earns the full benefit.
2. Temporary: at the time of application, have contributed at least 3 of the
   past 6 years (full benefit).
3. Transition (near-retirees): born before January 1, 1968 — partial benefit
   of 10% of the full benefit amount per contribution year (currently $3,650
   per year contributed), with a minimum of 1 year of contributions.

Other requirements:

- A qualifying year requires working at least 500 hours (~10 hours/week)
  (RCW 50B.04.050: "at least 500 hours during each of the ten years" or
  "each of the three years").
- Care-need trigger: needing help with 3 or more activities of daily living,
  with the need expected to continue at least 90 days. Counted ADLs: bathing,
  bed mobility, eating, medication management, mobility, transferring,
  toileting. Assistance includes supervision (monitoring, cueing, or set-up).
- Residency: workers who move out of state can choose to keep participating
  (including self-employed opt-ins) if they opt in within 1 year of leaving
  and have contributed at least 3 years at 500+ hours/year; per
  wacaresfund.wa.gov, "Benefits will be available to out-of-state
  participants who meet contribution requirements and need care starting in
  July 2030." Until then, benefits can only be received by participants
  living in Washington (no page states a residency rule verbatim; this
  follows from the July 2030 out-of-state start date).
- Workers with an approved WA Cares exemption cannot qualify.

## wa-cares-fund#01-what-it-covers

*Program: wa-cares-fund · Heading: What it covers*

## What it covers

- Lifetime benefit of $36,500 (2026), indexed to inflation annually, per
  person.
- WA Cares pays approved providers directly — no out-of-pocket payment for
  covered services; each service has a maximum rate providers cannot exceed or
  balance-bill.
- Covered service categories: in-home personal care; home-delivered meals;
  housework, errands, and yard work; adaptive equipment; home modifications;
  safety evaluations; personal emergency response systems; adult day services;
  transportation to medical appointments; care transition coordination;

# Medicaid Alternative Care (MAC)

MAC supports the unpaid family caregivers of people age 55+ who are already on
Apple Health (Medicaid), meet nursing-facility level of care, and have chosen
not to use the state's other Medicaid long-term services and supports.

**Program status: enrollment has been paused statewide since December 1,
2025**, with a first-come, first-served statewide waitlist — the same pause
that applies to TSOA (see wa-tsoa). Current participants keep their services;
no reopen date is announced.

## Eligibility

The care receiver must meet ALL of the following (DSHS LTC Manual Ch. 30b):

- Age 55 or older; the unpaid caregiver must be 18 or older.
- Lives in their own home or another person's home (not a licensed
  residential facility).
- Currently enrolled in Categorically Needy (CN) or Alternative Benefit Plan
  (ABP) Apple Health — regular Medicaid financial eligibility; there is no
  separate MAC income or resource test.
- Meets nursing facility level of care (NFLOC) but has chosen not to receive
  Medicaid LTSS through other programs (CFC, MPC, waivers).
- No client responsibility (cost-sharing); the DSHS manual notes MAC avoids
  the estate-recovery exposure that comes with other Medicaid LTSS.
- Changes must be reported within 30 days.
- Confirmed by rule: WAC 182-513-1605 (effective 6/28/2025, WSR 25-12-036)
  requires the person to "Be age 55 or older," "Be assessed as meeting
  nursing facility level of care under WAC 388-106-0355, and choose to
  receive services under the MAC program," live at home, meet Medicaid
  eligibility for noninstitutional CN/ABP coverage (or SSI-related with
  spousal impoverishment protections), and "Have an eligible unpaid
  caregiver under WAC 388-106-1905."

  age 18 or older" and participates in the required care planning
  (TCARE screening/assessment and care plan by step). The rule imposes no
  family-relationship test — DSHS materials say "family caregiver," but the

## wa-medicaid-cfc#00-overview-eligibility

*Program: wa-medicaid-cfc · Heading: Overview / Eligibility*

# Medicaid Community First Choice (CFC)

Washington's Medicaid State Plan option under §1915(k) of the Social Security
Act — the main vehicle for Medicaid-funded personal care for people who meet
an institutional level of care but live in the community. Administered
through Home and Community Services (aging/adult clients) and DDA.

## Eligibility

- Age: 18+ for services through HCS/AAA (DDA serves younger clients and
  refers adults without a DD determination to HCS).
- Functional: CARE assessment finding nursing facility level of care per WAC
  388-106-0355(1) (or ICF/IID level via DDA rules), or likelihood of needing
  institutional care within 30 days without services. To continue CFC at
  reassessment, the client must have an unmet or partially met need in at
  least three ADLs.
- Financial: must be eligible for Categorically Needy (CN) or Alternative
  Benefit Plan (ABP) coverage in the community — regular community Medicaid,
  not institutional financial rules. Clients already active on MAGI-based
  Apple Health (except Alien Emergency Medical) do not need to submit an
  additional financial application — only the functional assessment.
  SSI/SSI-related CN clients also qualify (standard SSI limits: income
  $994/$1,491, resources $2,000/$3,000).
- Cost: in an alternate living facility, CFC clients keep a personal needs
  allowance of $108.74 and pay room and board of $885.26 (1/1/2026).
  Per WAC 182-513-1215 (CFC eligibility), "post-eligibility treatment of
  income rules do not apply" to clients eligible through a noninstitutional
  CN/ABP program or spousal-impoverishment institutional-spouse status — so
  in-home CFC clients in those categories pay no participation toward cost
  of care; clients eligible through an HCB waiver remain subject to the
  waiver's post-eligibility (participation) rules.
- Federal basis (42 CFR 441.500): the subpart "implements section 1915(k)
  of the Act, referred to as the Community First Choice option," which
  makes "home and community-based attendant services and supports"
  available "to eligible individuals, as needed" — a Medicaid state plan
  option, meaning services are available to everyone who meets the
  eligibility rules rather than capped by waiver slots.

# Respite care programs in Washington

Short-term relief care for unpaid family caregivers, available through three
main channels: Lifespan Respite Washington vouchers, the Family Caregiver
Support Program (FCSP), and — only when the care receiver has a developmental
disability — DDA waiver respite. These programs generally do not stack:
Lifespan vouchers exclude people already receiving respite from another
publicly funded program (with a 30-day waitlist exception).

## Eligibility

Lifespan Respite Washington vouchers (administered by PAVE under a DSHS/ACL
grant):

- Unpaid caregiver (not paid to provide the care) providing 40+ hours/week of
  care or supervision.
- Caregiver and care receiver both live in Washington.
- Cannot afford respite on their own.
- Not enrolled in another program providing respite (unless waitlisted with
  no services scheduled within 30 days).
- Caregivers of all ages; no age restriction on the care receiver.

Family Caregiver Support Program (FCSP):

- Unpaid caregivers of adults age 18+ who need care. Free or low cost;
  eligibility details and services vary by community/Area Agency on Aging.
- Service level is tailored through a TCARE screening/assessment by a
  caregiver specialist.

DDA respite (only if the elder has a developmental disability — which
generally must have originated before age 18):

- Respite ("Break to the Caregiver") is a covered service under DDA HCBS
  waivers, including the Individual and Family Services (IFS) waiver for
  DDA-eligible people living in the family home.

# Tailored Supports for Older Adults (TSOA)

TSOA supports people age 55+ who need nursing-facility-level care but are not
on classic Medicaid — with financial limits well above regular Medicaid — and
their unpaid family caregivers. It also serves eligible individuals who have
no unpaid caregiver.

**Program status: enrollment has been paused statewide since December 1,
2025.** New applicants go on a first-come, first-served statewide waitlist
(WAC 388-106-1975); presumptive-eligibility screenings are suspended; current
participants keep their services; there is no announced reopen date. TSOA is
not an entitlement — funding is capped under the Section 1115 Medicaid
Transformation waiver (through June 30, 2028).

## wa-tsoa#01-eligibility

*Program: wa-tsoa · Heading: Eligibility*

## Eligibility

- Care receiver age 55+; the unpaid caregiver (in a caregiver/receiver
  "dyad") must be 18+. TSOA also serves individuals 55+ with no unpaid
  caregiver.

  Medicaid amounts. Applicants on Medically Needy or Medicare Savings Program

- Functional: nursing facility level of care (NFLOC) per WAC
  388-106-1900–1990.
- Financial (HCA income and resource standards effective April 1, 2026):
  - Income: gross income at or below 400% of the SSI federal benefit rate =
    $3,976/month (as of 1/1/2026; FBR single = $994).
  - Resources, single: at or below $84,354 (1/1/2026; six times the average
    monthly private nursing facility rate).
  - Resources, married with a community spouse: $156,883 (1/1/2026).
  - Standards update each January and every odd year in July.
- 12-month certification with continuous eligibility unless the person loses
  NFLOC, leaves Washington, enters an institution, becomes CN/ABP Medicaid
  eligible, or dies.
- No client responsibility (cost-sharing), and no estate-recovery exposure of
  the kind associated with other Medicaid LTSS.

## wa-tsoa#02-what-it-covers

*Program: wa-tsoa · Heading: What it covers*

## What it covers

Three benefit steps (the benefit belongs to the care receiver; in dyads the
services support the unpaid caregiver):

- Step 1: $250, one-time.
- Step 2: $500 annually (minus any Step 1 spending); requires a TCARE/GetCare
  screening. An exception-to-rule can extend to $1,000 (local approval) or
  beyond (HQ approval).
- Step 3: for 07/01/2025–06/30/2026, "an average of $928 per month not to
  exceed $5,568 in a six month period" (DSHS All HCS Rates document; an
  earlier printing of the same 2025–26 table showed $880/$5,280, and the
  2024–25 level was $844/$5,064). Requires a full TCARE/GetCare assessment;
  benefit levels are set under WAC 388-106-1920 and recalculated
  periodically from home care agency rates.

Service categories: Caregiver Assistance Services (dyads only — respite,
housekeeping and errands, adult day care, home-delivered meals); Personal
Assistance Services (individuals without a caregiver only — personal care
including nurse delegation, personal emergency response systems,
home-delivered meals); Training & Education; Specialized Medical Equipment &
Supplies; and Health Maintenance & Therapy Supports (for example counseling,
massage therapy).

## wa-tsoa#03-how-to-apply

*Program: wa-tsoa · Heading: How to apply*

## How to apply

- Online: washingtonconnection.org (Washington Connection portal).
- Paper: TSOA application HCA 18-008 — mail to DSHS Home and Community
  Services – Long Term Care Services, PO Box 45826, Olympia, WA 98504-5826;
  fax 1-855-635-8305; or deliver to a local Home & Community Services (HCS)
  office.
- Community Living Connections / Area Agency on Aging: waclc.org or
  1-855-567-0252 (ask for a Family Caregiver specialist).
- Functional eligibility is assessed by AAA/HCS staff; financial eligibility
  by HCS/HCA financial workers; eligibility is reviewed annually.
- Enrollment-pause intake (manually verified 2026-07-05): intake is
  waitlist-only — no new applications are financially processed for
  immediate services during the pause. Presumptive Eligibility screenings
  are suspended while the waitlist is in effect; submitting an application
  now only establishes the request date on the statewide first-come,
  first-served waitlist (managed by request date under WAC 388-106-1975),
  and full financial and functional assessments happen when the person is
  called from the waitlist. People fully enrolled or already approved for
  Presumptive Eligibility before December 1, 2025 keep their services and
  continue financial processing. While waiting, families can ask their AAA
  to screen for non-Medicaid FCSP respite funds that don't depend on the
  paused TSOA budget.

## wa-tsoa#04-documentation-needed

*Program: wa-tsoa · Heading: Documentation needed*

## Documentation needed

Per the TSOA application (HCA 18-008):

- Social Security number (or immigration document number), birthdate, marital
  status, immigration status.
- Income with proof: paystubs or employer statements; award letters; pension,
  annuity, or trust statements; VA, L&I, or railroad benefits; rental income.
- Resources with proof: bank statements, stocks and bonds, retirement
  accounts, life insurance, property, vehicles, trusts and annuities, funeral
  arrangements (personal effects excluded).
- Spouse's information if married, even if the spouse is not applying.
- Applicants have 10 calendar days to supply requested additional
  information; a written decision generally issues within 45 days.
