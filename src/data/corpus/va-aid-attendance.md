# VA Aid & Attendance (increased monthly pension)

Aid & Attendance (A&A) is an increased monthly pension amount added to a VA
pension for veterans (and some survivors) who need help with daily activities.
It cannot be received at the same time as the VA Housebound benefit — a
claimant gets one or the other.

## Eligibility

Two layers: the person must qualify for the underlying VA pension, and meet at
least one A&A clinical criterion.

Underlying Veterans Pension:

- Discharge that was not dishonorable.
- Income and net worth within limits set by Congress; the net worth limit is
  $163,699 (effective December 1, 2025).
- Service requirement: at least 90 days of active duty with at least 1 day
  during a recognized wartime period (if service began before September 8,
  1980); or at least 24 months / the full called period with at least 1 day
  during wartime (if enlisted after September 7, 1980); or officer rules for
  active duty starting after October 16, 1981.
- And at least one of: age 65 or older; permanent and total disability;
  long-term nursing home residence due to disability; receiving SSDI or SSI.
- Recognized wartime periods include WWII (12/7/1941–12/31/1946), Korean
  Conflict (6/27/1950–1/31/1955), Vietnam era (8/5/1964–5/7/1975, earlier for
  in-country service), and Gulf War (8/2/1990–ongoing).

A&A clinical criteria — at least one must apply:

- Needs another person to help perform daily activities such as bathing,
  feeding, or dressing.
- Has to stay in bed, or spends a large portion of the day in bed, because of
  illness.
- Is a patient in a nursing home due to loss of mental or physical abilities
  related to a disability.
- Eyesight limited to 5/200 or less in both eyes, or concentric contraction of
  the visual field to 5 degrees or less.

## What it covers

- A monthly cash payment added to the VA pension — not a service program. The
  money can be used for care costs at the household's discretion.
- Maximum Annual Pension Rate (MAPR) with A&A, effective December 1, 2025: the
  VA pays the difference between countable income and MAPR, divided into
  monthly payments.
  - Veteran with no dependents, with A&A: $29,093/year (versus $17,441
    standard).
  - Veteran with 1 dependent, with A&A: $34,488/year (versus $22,839
    standard).
  - Add $2,984 per additional dependent.
- Per VA's rates page, "We'll base your payment amount on the difference
  between your income for VA purposes and a limit that Congress sets
  (called the Maximum Annual Pension Rate, or MAPR)." VA publishes annual
  MAPR figures; per-month dollar amounts quoted elsewhere are derived from
  the annual figures, not published by VA.
- Surviving spouses: VA's Survivors Pension "offers monthly payments to
  qualified surviving spouses and unmarried dependent children of wartime
  Veterans who meet certain income and net worth limits set by Congress."
  Survivor MAPR with Aid & Attendance, no dependents: $18,697 (effective
  December 1, 2025).

## How to apply

- Underlying pension: VA Form 21P-527EZ — file online at VA.gov, upload via
  AccessVA QuickSubmit, mail, in person at a VA regional office, or through an
  accredited attorney, claims agent, or Veterans Service Organization. An
  intent-to-file is recommended for paper filers to preserve the effective
  date.
- A&A add-on: VA Form 21-2680 (Examination for Housebound Status or Permanent
  Need for Regular Aid and Attendance), with the examination section completed
  by a medical examiner.
- Mail applications to: Department of Veterans Affairs, Pension Intake Center,
  PO Box 5365, Janesville, WI 53547-5365. In person: any VA regional office.

## Documentation needed

- VA Form 21-2680 completed by a medical examiner.
- VA Form 21-0779 (Request for Nursing Home Information) if the person is in a
  nursing home.
- For the underlying pension: Social Security number, VA file number, military
  service history, marital and work history, dependent information, gross
  monthly household income, asset values, and unreimbursed medical expenses;
  medical records if under age 65.
