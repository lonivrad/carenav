# Apple Health long-term care (institutional/LTSS Medicaid)

Washington's Medicaid coverage for long-term services and supports — nursing
facility care and the financial gateway to HCS waivers (COPES, New Freedom,
Residential Support), PACE, hospice in an institution, and community LTSS.
Administered by the Health Care Authority (financial) and DSHS Home and
Community Services (functional and case management).

## Eligibility

General (WAC 182-513-1316/-1317):

- Meet Apple Health general requirements (WAC 182-503-0505), including
  citizenship/immigration rules.
- Attain institutional status (WAC 182-513-1320, effective 2/17/2017): in a
  medical institution, "a person must reside in a medical institution thirty
  consecutive days or more, or based on a department assessment, be likely
  to reside in a medical institution thirty consecutive days or more";
  outside an institution, status comes from receiving HCB waiver services
  (chapter 182-515 WAC) or equivalent programs (RCL, PACE, hospice, or
  state-funded LTC services).
- Meet functional eligibility — for HCS clients, nursing facility level of
  care via the CARE assessment (chapter 388-106 WAC).
- Meet SSI-related criteria (WAC 182-512-0050) or MAGI-based criteria if
  residing in a medical institution.

Income:

- SSI-related CN: available income at or below the Special Income Level
  (SIL), equal to 300% of the SSI federal benefit rate — $2,982/month
  (1/1/2026; FBR $994 single / $1,491 couple).
- Above the SIL: Medically Needy pathway (MNIL $994 single, 1/1/2026).
  Mechanics per WAC 182-513-1395 (institutional MN, effective 2/17/2017):
  a person whose remaining income exceeds the state-contracted daily rate
  but falls below the private nursing facility rate "is eligible to receive
  institutional services at the state-contracted rate," approved for a
  three- or six-month base period, with MN eligibility when "additional
  medical expenses incurred during the base period exceeds the total
  remaining income for all months of the base period minus the total
  state-contracted rate." Current private nursing facility rate: $462/day,
  $14,059/month (10/1/2025).
- MAGI-based institutional coverage (chapter 182-514 WAC) has no asset test.

Resources (SSI-related):

- $2,000 single / $3,000 couple; home equity limit $1,130,000 (1/1/2026) —
  exceeding it bars LTC coverage.
- Spousal impoverishment: community spouse resource allowance — state amount
  $72,529 (7/1/2025), federal maximum $162,660 (1/1/2026); community spouse
  monthly maintenance needs allowance maximum $4,066.50 (1/1/2026); dependent
  allowance $2,644 (7/1/2025). Resources above the $2,000 limit must be
  transferred to the community spouse within one year.
- 60-month look-back for asset transfers (WAC 182-513-1363): uncompensated
  transfers are presumed disqualifying unless rebutted or excepted (transfers
  to a spouse, to a blind or disabled child, sole-benefit trusts for a
  disabled person under 65, fair-market-value sales). Annuities must be
  disclosed (chapter 182-516 WAC).

Post-eligibility (cost of care): institutionalized clients pay income toward
cost of care minus a personal needs allowance — $41.62 (clothing/personal
incidentals) or $108.74 ("all other PNA, medical institutions," 1/1/2026);
State Veterans Home max $160. A housing maintenance allowance (up to 100% FPL,
$1,330, 4/1/2026) may be allowed to hold a residence.
Which PNA applies depends on setting and payment source (WAC 182-513-1105,
effective 12/5/2025, WSR 25-22-083 — separate amounts for medical
institutions vs. alternate living facilities and by funding source); "the
current PNA and room and board standards used in long-term services and
supports are published under the institutional standards on the Washington
apple health (medicaid) income and resource standards chart located at
www.hca.wa.gov."

## What it covers

- Nursing facility care for those meeting NFLOC — per DSHS's current
  long-term care residential options page, nursing homes provide "24-hour
  supervised nursing care, personal care, therapy, nutrition management,
  organized activities, social services, room, board and laundry."
- Gateway to: HCS home-and-community-based waivers (COPES, New Freedom,
  Residential Support Waiver), PACE, hospice in a medical institution, Roads
  to Community Living, and community LTSS (CFC, MPC, alternate living
  facility coverage).
- Full Apple Health CN medical benefits accompany LTC coverage.
- Estate recovery applies to LTSS paid from age 55 on.
- All dollar figures cited here are from HCA form 19-0096 (04/2026) and
  change annually — re-verify at use time.

## How to apply

Financial application (any one):

- Online: washingtonconnection.org (for people 65+, blind, disabled, or on
  Medicare).
- Online: wahealthplanfinder.org (under 65, no Medicare; flag the LTSS need
  in Additional Questions).
- Paper: HCA 18-005 "Washington Apple Health Application for Aged, Blind,
  Disabled / Long-Term Care" — mail or fax to Home and Community Services –
  LTSS, PO Box 45826, Olympia, WA 98504-5826; fax 1-855-635-8305.
- By phone via the DSHS customer service contact center, or in person at any
  HCS office or Community Services Office.

Functional assessment — call HCS central intake:

- Region 1 (Eastern WA, incl. Spokane, Yakima, Tri-Cities): 509-568-3767 or
  866-323-9409.
- Region 2 (Island, San Juan, Skagit, Snohomish, Whatcom, parts of North
  King): 800-780-7094; King County other ZIPs: 206-341-7750.
- Region 3 (Pierce, Kitsap, Thurston, Clark, Olympic Peninsula / SW WA):
  800-786-3799.

Process: (1) application; (2) financial review plus a required phone or
in-person interview with an HCS financial specialist (WAC 182-503-0040); (3)
in-person CARE assessment by an HCS social worker. Financial and functional
eligibility are determined concurrently; services start once both are
established and an approved provider is in place. Processing standard: 45
calendar days (60 if a disability decision is required, WAC 182-503-0060).
Denials carry written appeal rights. An authorized representative can be
added (DSHS 14-532). A "Fast Track" service authorization pending financial
determination exists.

## Documentation needed

- Social Security number and proof of identification.
- Proof of income.
- Documentation of resources: bank statements, property tax statements, life
  insurance.
- Immigration documents; proof of citizenship if not on Medicare or SSI.
- Verification of any transfers, gifts, or property sales within the 5-year
  look-back.
- Verification of unpaid medical expenses (especially the last 3 months) and
  other health coverage (HCA 14-194 for third-party/LTC insurance).
- Asset Verification System authorization (DSHS 27-189) and consent/release
  (DSHS 14-012 or HCA 80-020).
- Situational: DSHS 14-501 Community Resource Declaration (spousal resource
  snapshot at institutionalization), burial fund forms (DSHS 14-539/14-540),
  LTC Partnership asset designation (DSHS 10-438), annuity documentation.
- For the assessment: medical records, diagnoses, medication list, physician
  information, existing supports, and care-setting preferences.
