# WA Cares Fund

Washington's public long-term care insurance program, funded by a 0.58% payroll
premium on Washington workers' wages. Contributions began July 1, 2023;
benefits became available statewide as of July 1, 2026 (the site banner now
reads "Benefits now available statewide").

## Eligibility

Contribution (vesting) pathways — a worker must meet one:

1. Permanent: contribute for at least 10 years (earns the full benefit).
   <!-- VERIFY --> The statute (RCW 50A.04) phrases this as 10 years "without
   interruption of five or more consecutive years"; the wacaresfund.wa.gov
   pages say only "at least 10 years." Check the RCW for the exact rule.
2. Temporary: at the time of application, have contributed at least 3 of the
   past 6 years (full benefit).
3. Transition (near-retirees): born before January 1, 1968 — partial benefit
   of 10% of the full benefit amount per contribution year (currently $3,650
   per year contributed), with a minimum of 1 year of contributions.

Other requirements:

- A qualifying year requires working at least 500 hours (~10 hours/week).
- Care-need trigger: needing help with 3 or more activities of daily living,
  with the need expected to continue at least 90 days. Counted ADLs: bathing,
  bed mobility, eating, medication management, mobility, transferring,
  toileting. Assistance includes supervision (monitoring, cueing, or set-up).
- Residency: must live in Washington to use benefits. Workers who move out of
  state may keep participating if they contributed at least 3 years and opt in
  within 1 year of leaving; out-of-state benefits start July 2030.
  <!-- VERIFY --> The out-of-state opt-in and July 2030 date are confirmed on
  wacaresfund.wa.gov; the in-state residency requirement for initial benefit
  use is implied by program structure but was not found verbatim on the
  fetched pages.
- Workers with an approved WA Cares exemption cannot qualify.

## What it covers

- Lifetime benefit of $36,500 (2026), indexed to inflation annually, per
  person.
- WA Cares pays approved providers directly — no out-of-pocket payment for
  covered services; each service has a maximum rate providers cannot exceed or
  balance-bill.
- Covered service categories: in-home personal care; home-delivered meals;
  housework, errands, and yard work; adaptive equipment; home modifications;
  safety evaluations; personal emergency response systems; adult day services;
  transportation to medical appointments; care transition coordination;
  professional nursing; dementia and behavioral supports; caregiver education;
  respite care; and residential care (assisted living, adult family homes,
  memory care, nursing homes).
- <!-- VERIFY --> Whether wages for family caregivers are payable (as opposed
  to caregiver education and respite) was not explicitly stated on the
  benefits page.

## How to apply

- Online: create a SecureAccess Washington account and add the "WA Cares
  Benefits Account" service (portal: account.wacaresfund.wa.gov).
- Phone: 844-CARE4WA (toll-free; language assistance available; applications
  accepted by phone).
- In person: through a local Area Agency on Aging (finder at
  washingtoncommunitylivingconnections.org).
- Process: create account → submit application with work history → receive a
  contribution determination letter → care-needs discussion/assessment (phone
  or in person) for the 3+ ADL requirement → approval letter stating the
  available benefit amount.

## Documentation needed

- Name, address, and date of birth.
- Social Security number or Taxpayer Identification Number (an option exists
  for applicants without an SSN).
- Work history information for contribution verification.
- Legal documents when establishing authorized-user access or applying as a
  legal representative.
