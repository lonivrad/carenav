# Respite care programs in Washington

Short-term relief care for unpaid family caregivers, available through three
main channels: Lifespan Respite Washington vouchers, the Family Caregiver
Support Program (FCSP), and — only when the care receiver has a developmental
disability — DDA waiver respite. These programs generally do not stack:
Lifespan vouchers exclude people already receiving respite from another
publicly funded program (with a 30-day waitlist exception).

## Eligibility

Lifespan Respite Washington vouchers (administered by PAVE under a DSHS/ACL
grant):

- Unpaid caregiver (not paid to provide the care) providing 40+ hours/week of
  care or supervision.
- Caregiver and care receiver both live in Washington.
- Cannot afford respite on their own.
- Not enrolled in another program providing respite (unless waitlisted with
  no services scheduled within 30 days).
- Caregivers of all ages; no age restriction on the care receiver.

Family Caregiver Support Program (FCSP):

- Unpaid caregivers of adults age 18+ who need care. Free or low cost;
  eligibility details and services vary by community/Area Agency on Aging.
- Service level is tailored through a TCARE screening/assessment by a
  caregiver specialist.

DDA respite (only if the elder has a developmental disability — which
generally must have originated before age 18):

- Respite ("Break to the Caregiver") is a covered service under DDA HCBS
  waivers, including the Individual and Family Services (IFS) waiver for
  DDA-eligible people living in the family home.

## What it covers

- Lifespan Respite vouchers: up to $1,000 per household per year (the year
  starts at application/notification). Award periods typically run 3–4 months
  (longer for summer camps); vouchers are limited and awarded by available
  funding and priority need — not guaranteed. Respite is delivered through
  contracted agencies statewide (in-home respite, adult day services, other
  options); the provider bills Lifespan Respite directly.
- FCSP: planned short-term care from a few hours to a few days — in-home, at
  an adult day center, or in a residential care facility (providers include
  home health agencies, adult family homes, adult day health/day care,
  nursing facilities, or family/friends/volunteers) — plus support groups,
  counseling, caregiver training, supplies and equipment, and one-on-one
  consultation.
  No statewide dollar or hour cap for FCSP respite is published; amounts
  vary by Area Agency on Aging and local budget. Per manually gathered AAA
  information (2026-07-05, unpublished — confirm with the AAA before
  relying on it): King County (Aging & Disability Services) allows up to
  $4,362 over a rolling 6-month period for high-burden caregivers,
  prioritized by TCARE assessment tier (Priority 1–2 caregivers, e.g.
  40+ hours/week co-resident care, get respite funds first; lower-priority
  applicants are deferred to support groups when funding is tight);
  Snohomish County (county Human Services with Homage Senior Services)
  ranges roughly $1,000–$4,362/year on a sliding fee scale with cost
  sharing at higher incomes and a "screening and hold" roster when funds
  are fully allocated. Other counties differ — ask the local AAA via
  Community Living Connections, 1-855-567-0252.
- DDA: respite under the IFS or other DDA waivers. IFS annual allocation
  levels per WAC 388-828-9140 (effective 10/18/2021, WSR 21-19-093):
  Level 1 "up to: $1,200," Level 2 "up to: $1,800," Level 3 "up to:
  $2,400," Level 4 "up to: $3,600" per year. Overnight Planned Respite
  Services (OPRS), per the DSHS DDA respite page: for adults 18+ living
  with family or primary caregivers, "provided in a community setting,
  such as a home or apartment," serving "only one person at a time," and
  "you may request OPRS up to fourteen days within a calendar year."

## How to apply

- Lifespan Respite vouchers: apply online (form linked from
  wapave.org/lifespan-respite-washington), by phone at 253-442-3214, or by
  email at getrespite@wapave.org. Steps: apply → email award notification
  (amount and dates) → choose a provider from the approved agency list →
  register with the provider and notify Lifespan Respite.
- FCSP: through the local Area Agency on Aging — find it via Community Living
  Connections at 1-855-567-0252 or waclc.org.
- DDA: (1) establish DDA eligibility (dshs.wa.gov/dda), then (2) request
  waiver services through the DDA Service and Information Request process
  with a Case Resource Manager.

## Documentation needed

- Lifespan Respite: the application form (multiple care recipients may be
  listed on one form; the award splits evenly).
  No proof documents or income verification are required — confirmed
  against the current application form itself (tfaforms.com/4694142,
  retrieved 2026-07-05): eligibility is self-reported through required
  yes/no certifications on the form (e.g., "I provide 40 or more hours of
  care, supervision, or monitoring per week" and "I receive respite care
  through Medicaid or other program that provides respite care"), with no
  attachments requested.
- FCSP: TCARE screening/assessment completed with a caregiver specialist;
  requirements vary by AAA.
- DDA: DDA eligibility determination records, then the waiver service
  request.
