# VA Housebound benefit (increased monthly pension)

The Housebound benefit is an increased monthly pension amount for veterans who
are substantially confined to their home because of a permanent disability. It
cannot be received at the same time as VA Aid & Attendance — a claimant gets
one or the other.

## Eligibility

- Must receive (or qualify for) a VA pension. The underlying pension
  eligibility — discharge status, income and net worth limits (net worth limit
  $163,699 effective December 1, 2025), wartime service requirements, and
  age/disability criteria — is the same as described in the VA Aid &
  Attendance document (see va-aid-attendance).
- Clinical criterion (VA's plain-language standard): the person spends most of
  their time in their home because of a permanent disability.
- Regulatory definition (38 CFR 3.351(d)): applies to a veteran rated
  permanently and totally disabled who either "(1) Has additional disability
  or disabilities independently ratable at 60 percent or more, separate and
  distinct from the permanent disability rated as 100 percent disabling and
  involving different anatomical segments or bodily systems, or (2) Is
  'permanently housebound' by reason of disability or disabilities...
  substantially confined to his or her dwelling and the immediate premises
  or, if institutionalized, to the ward or clinical area, and it is
  reasonably certain that the disability or disabilities and resultant
  confinement will continue throughout his or her lifetime."

## What it covers

- A monthly cash payment added to the VA pension.
- Maximum Annual Pension Rate (MAPR) with Housebound status, effective
  December 1, 2025:
  - Veteran with no dependents: $21,313/year (versus $17,441 standard).
  - Veteran with 1 dependent: $26,710/year (versus $22,839 standard).
  - Add $2,984 per additional dependent.
- Two veterans married to each other (MAPR, effective December 1, 2025):
  one qualifies for Housebound: $26,710/year; both qualify for Housebound:
  $30,580/year; one qualifies for Housebound and one for Aid & Attendance:
  $38,350/year.

## How to apply

- Same process and forms as Aid & Attendance: underlying pension via VA Form
  21P-527EZ; Housebound status documented with VA Form 21-2680 (the same form
  covers both Housebound and A&A examinations).
- Submit online, by mail to: Department of Veterans Affairs, Pension Intake
  Center, PO Box 5365, Janesville, WI 53547-5365, or in person at a VA
  regional office.

## Documentation needed

- VA Form 21-2680 with the examination section completed by a medical
  examiner, documenting housebound status.
- VA Form 21-0779 (Request for Nursing Home Information) if in a nursing home.
- Underlying pension documentation: Social Security number, VA file number,
  military service history, marital and work history, dependent information,
  income, assets, and unreimbursed medical expenses.
