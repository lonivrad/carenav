# Medicaid Alternative Care (MAC)

MAC supports the unpaid family caregivers of people age 55+ who are already on
Apple Health (Medicaid), meet nursing-facility level of care, and have chosen
not to use the state's other Medicaid long-term services and supports.

**Program status: enrollment has been paused statewide since December 1,
2025**, with a first-come, first-served statewide waitlist — the same pause
that applies to TSOA (see wa-tsoa). Current participants keep their services;
no reopen date is announced.

## Eligibility

The care receiver must meet ALL of the following (DSHS LTC Manual Ch. 30b):

- Age 55 or older; the unpaid caregiver must be 18 or older.
- Lives in their own home or another person's home (not a licensed
  residential facility).
- Currently enrolled in Categorically Needy (CN) or Alternative Benefit Plan
  (ABP) Apple Health — regular Medicaid financial eligibility; there is no
  separate MAC income or resource test.
- Meets nursing facility level of care (NFLOC) but has chosen not to receive
  Medicaid LTSS through other programs (CFC, MPC, waivers).
- No client responsibility (cost-sharing); the DSHS manual notes MAC avoids
  the estate-recovery exposure that comes with other Medicaid LTSS.
- Changes must be reported within 30 days.

## What it covers

Benefit steps identical in structure to TSOA (the benefit attaches to the
care receiver's case; services support the caregiver):

- Step 1: $250, one-time.
- Step 2: $500/year (minus Step 1 spending); TCARE/GetCare screening
  required.
- Step 3: average $844/month, max $5,064 per six-month period (as of
  7/1/2024). <!-- VERIFY --> Current Step 3 amount is recalculated from home
  care agency rates (HCS Rates website, "MAC-TSOA" tab).

Categories for MAC dyads: Caregiver Assistance Services (respite care,
housekeeping and errands, adult day care / adult day health, home-delivered
meals — up to 2/day and 62/month per person without an exception); Training &
Education; Specialized Medical Equipment & Supplies; Health Maintenance &
Therapy Supports (counseling, support groups, massage). Personal Assistance
Services are NOT available under MAC (TSOA individuals only).

TCARE/GetCare screenings occur at least every 6 months; the benefit budget
runs in six-month periods.

## How to apply

- Because the care receiver is already on Apple Health, entry is through the
  local Community Living Connections / Area Agency on Aging office
  (waclc.org, 1-855-567-0252) or an HCS office; AAA staff complete the TCARE
  screening/assessment and enrollment.
- Washington Connection (washingtonconnection.org) is the portal for the
  underlying Apple Health/LTSS applications.
- During the pause, contacting the AAA/HCS provides options counseling and,
  on request, placement on the statewide waitlist.

## Documentation needed

- Proof of the care receiver's current CN/ABP Apple Health enrollment.
- Basic demographics for caregiver and care receiver.
- TCARE caregiver screening/assessment and a CARE (NFLOC) assessment
  completed by AAA/HCS staff.
- To join the current waitlist: basic demographic information plus whether
  there is an identified unpaid family caregiver.
