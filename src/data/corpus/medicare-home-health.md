# Medicare home health benefit

Medicare Part A and/or Part B cover part-time skilled care delivered at home by
a Medicare-certified home health agency, at no cost to the beneficiary for the
covered services themselves.

## Eligibility

All of the following are required:

- Need for part-time or intermittent skilled services. A person who needs more
  than part-time or intermittent skilled care does not qualify.
- Homebound status — both conditions: (1) leaving home is not recommended
  because of the condition, or the person has trouble leaving without help
  (cane, wheelchair, walker, crutches, special transportation, or another
  person) because of illness or injury; and (2) the person is normally unable
  to leave home and leaving takes considerable effort. Permitted absences:
  medical treatment and short or infrequent non-medical trips (for example,
  religious services); attending adult day care does not disqualify.
- A face-to-face assessment by a health care provider (physician or, for
  example, a nurse practitioner) before certification of need.
- The care must be ordered by a health care provider and delivered by a
  Medicare-certified home health agency.

## What it covers

- Medically necessary part-time or intermittent skilled nursing care: wound
  care for pressure sores or surgical wounds, patient and caregiver education,
  IV or nutrition therapy, injections, and monitoring of serious illness or
  unstable health status.
- Physical therapy, occupational therapy, and speech-language pathology
  services (subject to conditions).
- Medical social services.
- Part-time or intermittent home health aide care (help walking, bathing and
  grooming, changing bed linens, feeding) — only while also receiving skilled
  nursing, PT, SLP, or OT.
- Injectable osteoporosis drugs for women meeting certain criteria.
- Durable medical equipment (DME) and medical supplies for home use.
- Frequency: no cap on visits while qualified; "part-time or intermittent"
  generally means skilled nursing plus aide services combined up to 8 hours a
  day and 28 hours a week (up to 35 hours a week for a short time if the
  provider deems it necessary).
- Not covered: 24-hour-a-day care at home, home meal delivery, homemaker
  services unrelated to the care plan, and custodial or personal care when it
  is the only care needed.
- Costs: $0 for covered home health services. For Medicare-covered DME, 20%
  of the Medicare-approved amount after the Part B deductible ($283/year in
  2026).

## How to apply

1. A provider performs the face-to-face assessment and orders the care.
2. The provider supplies a list of Medicare-certified agencies serving the
   area (and must disclose any financial interest in agencies listed).
3. The agency schedules an appointment, discusses needs, and coordinates the
   care plan with the doctor.
4. Before care starts, the agency must state — verbally and in writing — what
   Medicare will and will not pay, and must issue an Advance Beneficiary
   Notice (ABN) before providing non-covered items or services.

## Documentation needed

- Face-to-face assessment and provider certification of homebound status and
  need for skilled care.
- Provider order for home health services and the agency-maintained care plan.
- <!-- VERIFY --> Medicare.gov does not publish an explicit checklist of
  paperwork the beneficiary must supply; the agency and provider assemble the
  certification records.
