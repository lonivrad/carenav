# Tailored Supports for Older Adults (TSOA)

TSOA supports people age 55+ who need nursing-facility-level care but are not
on classic Medicaid — with financial limits well above regular Medicaid — and
their unpaid family caregivers. It also serves eligible individuals who have
no unpaid caregiver.

**Program status: enrollment has been paused statewide since December 1,
2025.** New applicants go on a first-come, first-served statewide waitlist
(WAC 388-106-1975); presumptive-eligibility screenings are suspended; current
participants keep their services; there is no announced reopen date. TSOA is
not an entitlement — funding is capped under the Section 1115 Medicaid
Transformation waiver (through June 30, 2028).

## Eligibility

- Care receiver age 55+; the unpaid caregiver (in a caregiver/receiver
  "dyad") must be 18+. TSOA also serves individuals 55+ with no unpaid
  caregiver.
- Medicaid enrollment is NOT required — income and resources can be above
  Medicaid amounts. Applicants on Medically Needy or Medicare Savings Program
  coverage may still qualify.
  <!-- VERIFY --> The exact WAC rule that the applicant must not be
  enrolled in CN or ABP Medicaid (WAC 182-513-1615) was not fetched directly;
  the DSHS manual implies it (becoming CN/ABP-eligible terminates TSOA).
- Functional: nursing facility level of care (NFLOC) per WAC
  388-106-1900–1990.
- Financial (HCA income and resource standards effective April 1, 2026):
  - Income: gross income at or below 400% of the SSI federal benefit rate =
    $3,976/month (as of 1/1/2026; FBR single = $994).
  - Resources, single: at or below $84,354 (1/1/2026; six times the average
    monthly private nursing facility rate).
  - Resources, married with a community spouse: $156,883 (1/1/2026).
  - Standards update each January and every odd year in July.
- 12-month certification with continuous eligibility unless the person loses
  NFLOC, leaves Washington, enters an institution, becomes CN/ABP Medicaid
  eligible, or dies.
- No client responsibility (cost-sharing), and no estate-recovery exposure of
  the kind associated with other Medicaid LTSS.

## What it covers

Three benefit steps (the benefit belongs to the care receiver; in dyads the
services support the unpaid caregiver):

- Step 1: $250, one-time.
- Step 2: $500 annually (minus any Step 1 spending); requires a TCARE/GetCare
  screening. An exception-to-rule can extend to $1,000 (local approval) or
  beyond (HQ approval).
- Step 3: an average of $844/month, not to exceed $5,064 per six-month
  period; requires a full TCARE/GetCare assessment.
  <!-- VERIFY --> The Step 3 figures are the "as of 7/1/2024" amounts printed
  in the April 2026 DSHS manual; the current amount is recalculated from home
  care agency rates on the HCS Rates website ("MAC-TSOA" tab).

Service categories: Caregiver Assistance Services (dyads only — respite,
housekeeping and errands, adult day care, home-delivered meals); Personal
Assistance Services (individuals without a caregiver only — personal care
including nurse delegation, personal emergency response systems,
home-delivered meals); Training & Education; Specialized Medical Equipment &
Supplies; and Health Maintenance & Therapy Supports (for example counseling,
massage therapy).

## How to apply

- Online: washingtonconnection.org (Washington Connection portal).
- Paper: TSOA application HCA 18-008 — mail to DSHS Home and Community
  Services – Long Term Care Services, PO Box 45826, Olympia, WA 98504-5826;
  fax 1-855-635-8305; or deliver to a local Home & Community Services (HCS)
  office.
- Community Living Connections / Area Agency on Aging: waclc.org or
  1-855-567-0252 (ask for a Family Caregiver specialist).
- Functional eligibility is assessed by AAA/HCS staff; financial eligibility
  by HCS/HCA financial workers; eligibility is reviewed annually.
- <!-- VERIFY --> During the enrollment pause it is unclear whether full
  applications are financially processed or intake is waitlist-only (the
  waitlist FAQ says joining requires only basic demographic information).

## Documentation needed

Per the TSOA application (HCA 18-008):

- Social Security number (or immigration document number), birthdate, marital
  status, immigration status.
- Income with proof: paystubs or employer statements; award letters; pension,
  annuity, or trust statements; VA, L&I, or railroad benefits; rental income.
- Resources with proof: bank statements, stocks and bonds, retirement
  accounts, life insurance, property, vehicles, trusts and annuities, funeral
  arrangements (personal effects excluded).
- Spouse's information if married, even if the spouse is not applying.
- Applicants have 10 calendar days to supply requested additional
  information; a written decision generally issues within 45 days.
