# Medicaid COPES waiver (Community Options Program Entry System)

Washington's largest 1915(c) home-and-community-based services waiver,
operated by DSHS Home and Community Services since 1982, serving 51,000+
clients in their own homes, adult family homes, and assisted living
facilities. Since July 1, 2015, personal care itself is delivered through
Community First Choice (CFC), so nearly all COPES clients are dually enrolled
"CFC+COPES" — COPES wraps additional waiver services around CFC for people
whose needs exceed what CFC provides.

## Eligibility

- Age/status: 18+ and blind or disabled under SSI criteria (WAC
  182-512-0050), or age 65+.
- Functional: the CARE (Comprehensive Assessment Reporting Evaluation)
  assessment must find nursing facility level of care (NFLOC) per WAC
  388-106-0355(1) — or that the person will likely need that level within 30
  days without waiver services. The client must choose community services
  over nursing facility care (documented on DSHS form 14-225, a federal
  requirement before services can be authorized).
  <!-- VERIFY --> Exact CARE-score thresholds constituting NFLOC (WAC
  388-106-0355 text) were not fetched.
- Financial — two pathways:
  - Already on SSI, an SSI-related noninstitutional CN Apple Health program,
    or Health Care for Workers with Disabilities → financially eligible; pays
    nothing toward cost of care (room and board only if in an alternate
    living facility).
  - Institutional rules (WAC 182-515-1508): available income at or below the
    Special Income Level (SIL) — 300% of the SSI federal benefit rate —
    $2,982/month (1/1/2026). Income above the SIL can still qualify if net
    income after allowed deductions is at or below the one-person Medically
    Needy Income Level ($994, 1/1/2026).
- Resources: $2,000 single / $3,000 couple (SSI standard). Spousal
  impoverishment protections: state spousal resource amount $72,529
  (7/1/2025); federal spousal resource maximum $162,660 (1/1/2026); community
  spouse maintenance needs allowance maximum $4,066.50/month (1/1/2026).
- Other bars: 60-month look-back for uncompensated asset transfers (WAC
  182-513-1363); home equity must not exceed $1,130,000 (1/1/2026); annuity
  interests must be disclosed.
- Cost-of-care participation under institutional rules: a single person at
  home keeps a personal needs allowance up to the SIL ($2,982); married
  living with a spouse keeps up to the one-person MNIL ($994); in a
  contracted alternate living facility, keeps $108.74 and pays room and board
  of $885.26 (both 1/1/2026); remaining income goes to cost of care after
  allowed deductions.

## What it covers

Waiver services layered on top of CFC personal care (WAC 388-106-0300/-0305):

- Adult day care and adult day health.
- Client support training / wellness education (chronic disease
  self-management, PEARLS, STAR-C, EnhanceFitness, music therapy; limited to
  20 hours per 6-month period) and behavior support training.
- Community Choice Guide (transition and stabilization help, including rental
  deposits, first month's rent, utility hookups, household goods).
- Community supports: goods and services.
- Environmental modifications.
- Home-delivered meals.
- Nursing services and skilled nursing.
- Specialized medical equipment and supplies.
- Transportation.

Settings: private residence, adult family home, or assisted living facility
(some services in-home only). Waiver services may not duplicate or replace
Medicaid, Medicare, or insurance benefits.

## How to apply

Two-track LTSS process (identical to Apple Health LTC — see
wa-apple-health-ltc for full detail):

1. Financial application HCA 18-005 — online via washingtonconnection.org,
   mail or fax to HCS-LTSS, PO Box 45826, Olympia, WA 98504-5826 (fax
   1-855-635-8305), or in person at an HCS office.
2. Call the regional HCS central intake line to request a CARE assessment by
   an HCS social worker.

Ongoing case management for in-home COPES clients is handled by Area Agencies
on Aging; providers must be contracted through the local AAA.

## Documentation needed

Same as the Apple Health LTC / LTSS application (see wa-apple-health-ltc),
plus:

- Signed DSHS 14-225 Acknowledgment of Services before waiver authorization.
- Annuity disclosure.
- Verification of any transfers, gifts, or property sales within the 5-year
  look-back.
